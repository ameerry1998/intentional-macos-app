Collapsing 1, 2, 7, 10 into one family you were right

Background tint (1), Wrapper container (2), Hatched pattern (7), Z-axis depth (10) all share the same idea: "routines as background, tasks as foreground." The visual treatment varies; the mental model is identical. Treat them as one family — call it the Overlay family. Pick one rendering and ship it (currently #1 background tint in today.html v4). The others are paint jobs, not paradigm shifts.

7 truly different paradigms not "calendar with paint"

Why this is different: routines and tasks live in separate spatial regions. Never overlay. Left panel = recurring scaffolding. Right panel = today's work. Each gets its own dedicated screen real estate.

Alt B — State Card hero (calendar collapsed)

Why this is different: there's no calendar by default. The page is dominated by ONE big card showing the current state ("In Deep Work · doing Outline blog #3 · 1h 22m left"). The full calendar is collapsed behind a "Today" expand. Inspired by Apple Watch — context-first, drilldown for detail.

My honest take across all 7

If you want fundamentally different feel, the strongest contenders are:

  • Alt B (State Card hero) — most striking. Most "different." Closest to the Apple Watch / premium wearable aesthetic. Risk: hides the calendar.
  • Alt C (Linear agenda story) — feels human, story-shaped, more emotional than a grid. Risk: less efficient for power users.
  • Alt E (Circular clock) — beautiful, brand-signature potential. Risk: editing is hard, needs the list panel.
  • Alt G (AI commentary stream) — most aligned with your "AI coach" vision for the product. Risk: requires very good AI copy or it feels gimmicky.

The "boring but solid" choice is still the Overlay family (1/2/7/10) — it's familiar, scales, works. The "I want to make a statement" choice is Alt B or Alt G. Pick the lane that matches the product's identity: tool (Overlay) vs. companion (AI stream) vs. premium watch (State card / Clock).
