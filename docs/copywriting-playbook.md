# Landing Page Copywriting Playbook for Intentional

## Executive Summary

Research-backed foundation for writing high-converting landing page copy for Intentional, a macOS distraction-blocking app for knowledge workers. Covers competitor messaging teardowns, credible distraction statistics, copywriting frameworks, headline formulas, psychological triggers, section-by-section guidance, common mistakes, pricing framing, CTA optimization, and competitive positioning.

## 1. Competitor Messaging Teardowns

| App | Headline / Tagline | Strategy |
|-----|-------------------|----------|
| **Freedom** | "Freedom lets you focus on what truly matters." | Aspirational identity |
| **Cold Turkey** | "The Toughest Website Blocker on the Internet" | Dominance/authority |

| **Opal** | "5 to 6 hours. That's the average time you'll spend looking at your phone today." | Shock statistic; guilt |

### Key Patterns
- Freedom anti-gamifies — Intentional should flip: earned browse IS gamification, but *aligned* gamification (rewarding productive behavior)
- Cold Turkey leads with bypass-prevention as core promise
- Opal weaponizes lifetime stat: "17 years staring at a phone"
- Superhuman anchors value to time saved
- NO competitor uses AI-based relevance scoring, progressive enforcement, or earned browse in messaging — this is open territory

## 2. Distraction Statistics (by emotional intensity)

### Existential / Guilt-Inducing (Hero + Stats)
- Average worker productive for only **2h 53min** per 8-hour day
- **40%** of knowledge workers never get 30 uninterrupted minutes in a workday
- Workers lose **720 hours/year** to distraction — 18 full working weeks
- Average daily social media: **2h 21min** globally — ~32 full days/year

### Economically Shocking (ROI / Pricing)
- Workplace distractions cost U.S. economy **$650 billion annually**
- Disengaged employees: **$8.9 trillion** in lost global GDP

### Cognitively Alarming (Problem Cards)
- **23 min 15 sec** to refocus after one interruption (Gloria Mark, UC Irvine)
- **15 interruptions per hour** — one every 4 minutes
- Knowledge workers toggle **1,200 times/day** between apps (HBR 2022)
- Distracted employees make **50% more errors**

### Emotionally Relatable (Above-the-fold)
- **79%** get distracted within 1 hour of starting a task
- **73.2%** feel overwhelmed due to constant interruptions
- **56%** say social media is top distraction

### Recommended Stat Hierarchy
1. Hero eyebrow: "You're productive for 2 hours and 53 minutes per day. The rest is noise."
2. Stats section (3 cards): 23-min refocus → 1,200 app switches/day → 50% more errors
3. Pricing ROI: "720 hours lost/year × your hourly rate = ?"

## 3. Copywriting Frameworks

### PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution) — Foundation
- **Problem**: "You've tried willpower. You've tried Screen Time. You've tried blocking apps. You still end up on Twitter at 2pm with a deadline at 4."
- **Agitate**: "Every distraction costs you 23 minutes of refocus time. A single moment of weakness costs half an hour. You're productive for less than 3 hours out of 8."
- **Solution**: "Intentional doesn't just block distractions. It understands what you're working on. Gentle nudge first, then grayscale, then full block — so you never feel punished, but you always get back on track."

### BAB (Before → After → Bridge) — Testimonials + Hero
- **Before**: "You open your laptop at 9am with a clear plan. By 10:30, you've checked Slack 14 times, opened Reddit 'for one second,' and now you're watching a YouTube video about why beavers are underrated."
- **After**: "Imagine finishing your deep work block and realizing you earned 20 minutes of guilt-free social media time."
- **Bridge**: "Intentional is the bridge. On-device AI scores activity in real time, progressive enforcement nudges before blocking, earned browse time makes focus rewarding."

### StoryBrand — Page Architecture
- **Character**: "You're ambitious. You know what you should be doing. But the internet keeps winning."
- **Problem**: External=distractions; Internal=guilt; Philosophical="It shouldn't be this hard to do work you care about."
- **Guide**: Empathy + authority
- **Plan**: 3 steps
- **Success**: "Ship the project. Finish the chapter. Reclaim your afternoons."
- **Failure**: "Or keep losing 720 hours a year to the scroll."

## 5. Emotional Triggers

### Loss Aversion (Most Powerful)
Frame focus as something being *taken*, not something to *acquire*. "You're losing 720 hours every year."

### Guilt / Self-Disappointment
Validate then absolve: "It's not a willpower problem. Apps are designed by thousands of engineers to steal your attention. You were never supposed to fight that alone."

### Identity / Aspiration
Tie usage to who customer wants to be. "Since using Intentional, I actually feel like the engineer I'm supposed to be."

### FOMO on Life
"How many of those years are you trading for your best work?"

### Social Proof
Place directly below hero CTA and again near pricing.

## 7. What NOT to Do

- "Boost your productivity" — generic, means nothing
- "Revolutionary" / "Game-changing" — hollow superlatives
- "We help you focus" — too much "we," not enough "you"
- "Powered by AI" — table stakes in 2026, specify what AI does
- "Simple and easy to use" — show, don't tell
- Leading with features instead of problems
- No social proof or social proof buried below fold
- Passive-aggressive opt-out copy ("No thanks, I love being distracted")
- Speaking to everyone instead of specific audience
- Walls of text — 79% of users scan

## 8. Pricing Copy

### Daily cost breakdown
$6/month = $0.20/day. Frame against cost of distraction, not other apps.

### Cost-of-distraction ROI
720 hours lost/year × $30/hour = $21,600. Intentional costs $72/year = less than 0.4% of what distraction costs.

### Lifetime psychology
"Pay once, focus forever." Position as the smart choice.

### Recommended structure
- Headline: "Less than the cost of one lost afternoon"
- Anchor the value: show cost of distraction BEFORE showing prices
- Risk reversal: free tier or 7-day trial

## 9. CTA Optimization

- Changing CTA copy alone increased conversions by 182% in one test
- "Start my free trial" > "Start your free trial" (+90%, Unbounce)
- Personalized CTAs perform 202% better than generic (HubSpot)
- "Download" outperforms "Get Started" for desktop apps
- Include platform: "Download for macOS"
- Adding "free" increased conversions by 15%
- Repeat primary CTA 4+ times across page

### Recommended CTAs
| Location | CTA |
|----------|-----|
| Hero (primary) | "Download Free for macOS" |

| Final CTA | "Download Intentional Free" |

## 10. Competitive Positioning

### The "Only We Do This" Moat
1. AI relevance scoring (not blocklists)
2. Progressive enforcement (nudge → grayscale → block)
3. Earned browse time (unique in category)
4. Accountability partners with strict mode
5. Block rituals / intention setting

### Positioning Statement
"Screen Time is too weak. Freedom is too dumb. Cold Turkey is too harsh. Intentional is the focus app that *understands* your work — nudging you gently, blocking when needed, and rewarding your focus with earned browse time."

### Attack Lines (Without Naming)
- vs Screen Time: "Built-in screen limits are easy to bypass and don't understand context."
- vs Freedom: "Blocklists treat all websites the same. Stack Overflow isn't Reddit."
- vs Cold Turkey: "All-or-nothing blocking works until you need a blocked site for work."
- vs one sec: "A pause screen is better than nothing. But if you still tap through every time, you need progressive enforcement."
