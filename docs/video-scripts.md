## Explainer Video — "How Intentional Works" (~75 seconds)

**HeyGen notes:** This can be a talking-head presenter OR you can do this as a voiceover with screen recordings of the actual app. If HeyGen, use a clean, professional avatar — friendly authority, not salesy. Consider splitting into presenter segments + screen capture overlays in post.

**[INTRO — 0:00-0:10]**

> You sit down to work. You know exactly what you need to do. Two hours later, you've checked Twitter six times, watched a YouTube video about fermentation, and now you're wondering where the morning went.
>
> Sound familiar? Here's how Intentional fixes that.

**[STEP 1: PLAN — 0:10-0:25]**

> When you open Intentional, you set your schedule for the day. Deep Work blocks for heads-down coding. Focus Hours for lighter work. Free Time for breaks.
>
> Quick Plan does it in under a minute — or you drag blocks on the calendar yourself.

**[STEP 2: FOCUS — 0:25-0:50]**

> Once a block starts, Intentional's AI watches what you're doing — completely on-device, nothing leaves your Mac.
>
> If you're on a site that's relevant to your task, it stays invisible. But the moment you drift — say you open Reddit during a coding block — it nudges you. A small "not related to your task" message appears.
>
> Still there after a minute? The screen starts going grayscale. Still there? It blocks it. Three levels. You always get a warning first.

**[STEP 3: EARN — 0:50-1:05]**

> Here's the part that makes it stick: every 5 minutes of focused work earns you 1 minute of guilt-free social media. You can literally watch your browse time building up.
>
> Deep Work earns even faster. So your hardest sessions are your most rewarding.

**[CLOSE — 1:05-1:15]**

> No willpower required. No blocklists to maintain. Just an AI that understands your work and keeps you honest.
>
> Download Intentional free for macOS.

## Production Notes

**For HeyGen testimonials:**
- Keep avatars diverse in appearance and setting
- Aim for "real person on a Zoom call" energy, not polished commercial
- If HeyGen lets you adjust pacing, slightly faster than default reads more natural
- Each video should be standalone — someone might only see one

**For the explainer:**
- Consider recording actual screen captures of the app (planning view, pill timer, nudge toast, grayscale effect, earned browse bar) and overlaying the voiceover
- The HeyGen avatar could appear for intro and close, with screen recordings for Steps 1-3
- Keep total runtime under 90 seconds — attention drops sharply after that

**Thumbnail suggestions for testimonial cards:**
- Frame the avatar from chest up, slight smile, looking at camera
- Each should have a different background/color feel
- Export as 16:9 ratio to match the `.testimonial-video` aspect ratio on the landing page
