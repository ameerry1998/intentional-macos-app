# Landing Page Teardowns & Lessons

Analysis of exactly.ai, Outseta, Solidroad, Granola, monday.com, Asana, and Mixpanel — plus the 7 conversion killers framework.

---

## Page-by-Page Breakdown

### Granola.ai — The Gold Standard

**Headline:** "The AI notepad that helps you get the doing done."

This is the best page of the seven. Here's why:

**1. Hero that sells an outcome, not a category.**
Not "AI meeting transcription platform." Not "intelligent note-taking software." "Helps you get the doing done." That's what changes in your life. The custom handwritten SVG on the word "doing" makes it feel crafted, not generated.

**2. Logo bar IMMEDIATELY after the hero.**
"Helping the world's best product teams focus more on their meetings" — then a scrolling marquee of PostHog, Intercom, Ramp, Linear, Index, Brex, Replit, Vercel. These aren't random companies. These are the exact companies their target audience (product teams, developers, founders) respects. This is social proof that self-selects.

**3. How It Works is TWO steps. Not three. Not five. Two.**
- Step 1: "Granola is like Apple Notes, but it also transcribes your meeting"
- Step 2: "When the meeting ends, Granola enhances the notes you've written"

That's it. The entire product explained in two sentences. Each step has an interactive demo showing the before/after. You don't need to think. You see it.

**4. Social proof section is DEVASTATING.**
Real tweets. Not polished testimonial quotes in cards. Actual tweets from Nat Friedman (former GitHub CEO), Guillermo Rauch (Vercel founder), Ryan Hoover (Product Hunt founder), Des Traynor (Intercom co-founder). With their real handles. Their real words:

- "It's actually unbelievable how good granola.ai is...There's no going back to pre-granola days" — Guillermo Rauch
- "I don't think I've ever gotten more thanks" — Des Traynor
- "Granola is one of the best made 'AI' apps...It's minimal, fast, with no setup. It. just. works" — Deedy

This is incomparably more credible than stock-photo testimonials or "coming soon" video placeholders.

**5. CTA is simple and low-pressure.**
"Try Granola for a few meetings today. It's free to get started." Not "Start your 7-day free trial." Not "Download now." Just... try it. For a few meetings. Today. Zero pressure.

**6. Feature section sells capabilities through questions.**
Instead of "AI-powered action item extraction," they show a carousel of things you can ask: "Write a follow up email," "List action items," "What's their budget?" "List their objections." These are the user's actual thoughts after a meeting. The feature IS the user's need.

**Lesson for Intentional:** The gap between Granola's page and ours is enormous. Their social proof alone would outconvert everything else on our page combined. We need real testimonials — even beta user quotes — not "coming soon" placeholders. Our "How It Works" should be as brutally simple as theirs.

### Outseta — The Before/After Master

**Headline:** "Launch and Scale Your Membership"

**Subheadline:** "Everything you need to grow, from payments and protected content to CRM and email."

**1. The Before/After section is the best thing on this page.**

Title: "Quit context switching"

**Before (labeled "Current Stack"):**
Lists 6 tools (Memberstack, Intercom, Mailchimp, Auth0, Hubspot, ChartMogul) and then describes the PAIN:
- "Constant tab switching"
- "A slew of tools duct-taped together"
- "Too much time spent integrating"
- "Scattered prospect and customer data"
- "A bunch of costly subscriptions"
- "Broken Zaps everywhere"
- "Extra fees for every feature or user"
- "Software that's only fractionally used"

**After (labeled "With Outseta"):**
- "One place to set your pricing"
- "One tool to set up email campaigns"
- "One app for segmentation"
- "One inbox for customer support"
- "One grid to see your customer data"
- "One chart to track your MRR"
- "One login to manage your whole business"
- "One simple price"

The repetition of "One" is powerful. The contrast between chaos (8 pain points) and simplicity (8 "one" statements) makes the value proposition visceral, not abstract.

**2. Social proof uses real Twitter/X posts with handles.**
Testimonials include actual X handles and can be verified:
- "$100K in the first 48 hours with outseta" — with link to original post
- "I can't believe how simple it is" — @ale_sannazzaro
- "We've walked away from Hubspot, Intercom and Chargebee" — Bryan Watson, CEO

**3. Use case carousel pre-qualifies visitors.**
Rotating pills: "Membership sites · SaaS products · Clubs · Associations · Courses · Communities." The visitor immediately knows if they're in the right place.

**4. Integration logos build trust.**
Webflow, Framer, WordPress, React, Circle, Squarespace, Notion, Discord, Supabase — 12+ logos showing where Outseta fits in your stack.

**Lesson for Intentional:** We could build a devastating before/after section. Before: "Screen Time, Freedom, Cold Turkey — 3 apps that treat every website the same, block the ones you need for work, and get uninstalled within a week." After: "One app that understands what you're working on. Nudges before it blocks. Rewards focus instead of punishing it. And your 3pm self can't disable it." The "one" repetition pattern works perfectly for positioning against multiple competitors.

### exactly.ai — Clean and Direct

**Headline:** "Train AI to replicate your brand style"

**Key lesson:** Verb-first headline. Not "An AI platform for brand consistency." "Train AI to replicate your brand style." The user is the subject. They're doing something. The headline is an action, not a description.

---

### monday.com — Enterprise Scale

**Headline:** "Outpace everyone with the best AI work platform"

**Key lessons:**
1. **Multi-product navigation** — Products dropdown shows Work Management, CRM, Campaigns, Dev, Service. They're not trying to explain everything on one page.
2. **Audience segmentation in nav** — "By Team" (Marketing, Sales, Product, IT) and "By Business Size" (Enterprise, Small Business, Nonprofit). The visitor self-selects before they even scroll.
3. **"Get Started" as primary CTA** — not "Download" or "Try Free." Simple, action-oriented.

---

### Solidroad — Clean Design Language

**Key lesson:** Dark green color palette creates immediate differentiation. The visual design IS the positioning. When every SaaS page looks the same (dark mode, purple/blue gradients), a strong color choice stands out.

## The 7 Conversion Killers (Applied to Intentional)

### 1. Hero says what the product IS, not what it DOES for the user.

**Our current hero sub:** "One tab becomes forty minutes. Every single day. Intentional is a macOS app that understands what you're actually working on — and keeps you there."

**Problem:** "Intentional is a macOS app that understands what you're actually working on" describes what the product IS. Better to describe what changes in the user's life.

**Fix:** Lead with the transformation. "One tab becomes forty minutes. Every single day. What if something actually caught you before the rabbit hole? And rewarded you for staying focused?"

### 2. No social proof above the fold.

**Our current page:** Zero social proof above the fold. No logos. No user counts. No testimonials. Nothing until the testimonial section, which says "Video testimonials — coming soon."

**"Coming soon" is WORSE than nothing.** It tells the visitor nobody uses this product yet.

**Fix:** Either get real testimonials (even 3 beta users), show a user count ("Join 500+ developers already focused"), or remove the testimonials section entirely until we have real ones. An empty social proof section actively hurts credibility.

### 3. Too many CTAs competing for attention.

**Our current page:** "Download Free for macOS" + "See How It Works" in the hero. This is actually fine — one primary, one secondary. But repeated inconsistently throughout the page.

**Fix:** Use ONE CTA pattern everywhere: "Download Free for macOS." The "See How It Works" can be an anchor link, not a styled button competing for attention.

### 4. Pricing page requires a "Contact Sales" click.

**Our current page:** Shows pricing clearly ($6/mo and $79 lifetime). This is correct. Don't hide it.

### 5. Feature list instead of benefit list.

**Our current Benefits section is actually decent** — "It understands your actual work" (benefit), "It warns before it blocks" (benefit), "Focus earns social media" (benefit), "Your 3pm self can't cheat" (benefit). These pass the "so what?" test.

**But the pricing features list is still features:** "AI that understands your work" is borderline. "Earned browse rewards" is a feature name. "Progressive enforcement" is jargon.

**Fix pricing features:** Instead of "Progressive enforcement" → "Warns before it blocks." Instead of "Schedule builder" → "Plan your day in 60 seconds."

### 6. No competitor comparison anywhere.

**Our current page:** No comparison. We removed it in v4.

**This is a mistake.** Per the research: "Your prospects ARE comparing you. If you do not help them compare (honestly), they will do it themselves on G2 where you cannot control the narrative."

**Fix:** Add back a comparison, but NOT as a checkbox grid (boring, feels defensive). Use Outseta's before/after format instead. Show the pain of the current tools, then the relief of Intentional.

### 7. Mobile experience is broken.

**Our current page:** Has responsive CSS for 900px and 600px breakpoints. Benefits grid goes 1-column. Pricing stacks. This is probably fine but needs manual testing.

## The 10 Biggest Lessons

### 1. Real social proof > no social proof > fake social proof
Granola has tweets from Guillermo Rauch and Nat Friedman. Outseta has "$100K in 48 hours" screenshots. Our page has "Video testimonials — coming soon." This is the single biggest credibility gap. Either get real quotes or remove the section entirely.

### 2. Before/After beats feature lists
Outseta's before/after section is worth more than any feature grid. The pain of "Broken Zaps everywhere" + "A slew of tools duct-taped together" is visceral. The relief of "One login to manage your whole business" is clear. We should use this pattern: "Before Intentional" (the chaos of distraction) vs "After" (the focused day).

### 3. Two-step How It Works beats three-step
Granola explains their entire product in 2 steps with interactive demos. Our 3-step is fine, but consider: can we reduce to 2? "Tell it what you're working on" + "It keeps you there and rewards your focus." Everything else is detail.

### 4. Show the product immediately
Every page shows their product within the first scroll. Screenshots, animations, interactive demos. Our video placeholder is just a dark rectangle with a play button. If we don't have a video yet, show an actual screenshot of the app — the pill timer, the calendar, the nudge in action.

### 5. Headlines are verbs, not nouns
"Train AI to replicate your brand style" (exactly.ai) — verb first. "Launch and Scale Your Membership" (Outseta) — verb first. "Outpace everyone" (monday) — verb first. Our headline "You know you're wasting time" is a confrontation, which works — but the sub should be a verb/action, not a description.

### 6. Logo bars are standard equipment
Granola, Outseta, monday — all have logo bars. It's expected. Without it, the page feels unvalidated. If we don't have company logos, we need SOME form of trust signal in or near the hero.

### 7. Testimonials should be raw, not polished
Granola's tweets with real handles and messy enthusiasm ("It. just. works." / "I can't imagine life without it") are 10x more credible than our placeholder video cards with stock-photo energy. When we get testimonials, make them raw — screenshots of DMs, tweets, Discord messages.

### 8. CTA language should be low-pressure and specific
Granola: "Try Granola for a few meetings today. It's free to get started." Outseta: "Sign up for free." Both are low-commitment. "Download Free for macOS" is decent. But "Try Intentional for one afternoon. It's free." might be even better because it specifies a time commitment.

### 9. Feature sections should use the user's language, not the product's
Granola shows "Write a follow up email" / "List action items" / "What's their budget?" — these are the user's literal thoughts. Not "AI-powered action item extraction." Our benefits cards are OK at this, but the pricing features list still uses our internal language.

### 10. Comparison should be emotional, not a checklist
Outseta doesn't do a checkbox grid. They show the FEELING of using 6 tools vs 1. "Constant tab switching" / "Broken Zaps everywhere" vs "One login." A before/after is more persuasive than checkmarks because it makes the reader feel the pain they're already living with.

## What Intentional's Page Is Missing (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Real social proof** — #1 priority. Get 3-5 real user quotes. Screenshots of messages. Anything real. Remove "coming soon" immediately.

2. **Before/After section** — Replace the removed comparison table with an Outseta-style before/after. "Before: Screen Time, Freedom, Cold Turkey..." / "After: One app that..."

3. **Product visuals in the hero** — Replace the video placeholder with an actual screenshot or animated GIF of the app in action. The nudge, the pill timer, the grayscale effect — these are the selling points and they're invisible.

4. **Competitor positioning** — Users ARE comparing. Help them. Not a boring checkbox table — an emotional before/after or a paragraph that honestly addresses "Why not Freedom? Why not Cold Turkey?"

5. **Lower-pressure CTA language** — "Try Intentional for one afternoon. It's free." is warmer than "Download Free for macOS."

6. **Simpler How It Works** — Consider 2 steps instead of 3. Or at minimum, make each step one sentence with a visual, not a paragraph.

## Applied: What the V5 Page Should Look Like

```
Nav (minimal — logo + How It Works + Pricing + FAQ + Download CTA)

Hero
  Badge: "For people who've tried every blocker and uninstalled them all"
  H1: "You know you're wasting time."
  Sub: Outcome-focused, verb-driven. What changes in their life.
  Product visual: Real screenshot or GIF, NOT a video placeholder
  CTA: "Try Intentional free" (primary) + "See how it works" (text link)
  Trust signal: User count, or a single powerful quote, or logos

Benefits (4 cards, 2x2)
  Benefit-focused, "so what?"-tested copy
  Each card answers: "What changes in my life?"

Before/After (NEW — replaces comparison table)
  Before: The chaos of current tools (specific pain points)
  After: Life with Intentional (specific relief)

How It Works (2-3 steps, stacked, text then visual)
  As simple as possible. One sentence per step. Interactive visual below.

Social Proof
  Real quotes only. No placeholders. Raw tweets/messages preferred.
  If no real quotes yet, SKIP this section entirely.

Pricing (simple, 2 cards)
  Feature list uses user language, not product language.
  Clear guarantee.

FAQ (6 items)
  Conversational, honest, addresses real objections.

Final CTA
  Emotional close. Low-pressure language.

Footer
```
