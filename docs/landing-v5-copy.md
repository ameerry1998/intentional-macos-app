## Section 8: FAQ

**Q: What if I need YouTube for work?**
That's literally why this exists. Intentional doesn't block YouTube. It scores each page against your task. A React tutorial while you're building in React? You're fine. A compilation of fails? You're getting nudged. Same domain, completely different treatment.

**Q: Can I really not turn it off?**
Up to you. Without Strict Mode, quit whenever you want. With it on? It survives Cmd+Q, relaunches on reboot, and needs your partner's 6-digit code. You pick which version of yourself makes the rules.

**Q: Is my data private?**
Everything stays on your Mac. The AI runs locally on Apple's Neural Engine. Your URLs, page titles, and browsing data never leave the device. No cloud. No account. No tracking.

**Q: Which browsers does it work with?**
Chrome, Safari, Arc, Brave, Edge, Firefox, Opera, and Vivaldi. Desktop apps — Slack, Figma, Xcode, whatever — are tracked natively too.

**Q: Does it slow down my Mac?**
Nope. AI runs on Apple's Neural Engine. Tab checks happen every 10 seconds. You'll forget it's running.

**Q: How is this different from Freedom?**
Freedom blocks sites. All of them, or none of them. Intentional understands what you're working on and treats the same URL differently depending on your task. Plus: earned browse time, progressive enforcement, and accountability that survives force-quit. Different animal.

## Notes on Tone

- Every sentence should sound like a person who's lived through distraction hell, not a marketing team.
- Sentence rhythm: short, long, short, fragment. Never metronomic.
- "You" in every paragraph. The reader is the subject, not the product.
- Specific > vague. "Cmd+Q" > "closing the app." "Reddit while debugging" > "off-task browsing."
- Honesty > hype. Acknowledge that people WILL test the boundaries. That's the point of Strict Mode.
- No AI buzzwords. "On-device AI" is stated once as a fact, not as a selling point. The selling point is what it DOES (understands your work), not what it IS (artificial intelligence).
