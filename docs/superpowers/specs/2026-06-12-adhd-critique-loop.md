# The ADHD Critique Loop — tearing down the v3 day until only the user is left

Method: take the v3 storyboard design, attack it from the lived ADHD experience, fix, attack again. Repeat until the attacks stop landing. The ICP is not "a user who needs blocking" — it's a brain where **starting is agony, time is invisible, interruption kills the only good hours, and shame is the #1 churn reason.**

---

## ROUND 1 — the morning ritual is still a productivity-app fantasy

**Design as it stands:** Mac opens → 4-screen ritual → approve a 6-block day grid.

**ADHD reality:**
- Nobody opens the Mac "to start the day." They open it mid-impulse — to check one thing. 47 restored tabs and a Discord ping are already screaming. A 4-screen ritual competing with restored chaos loses before screen 2.
- **Approving a 9-to-5 block grid at 8:40 is a trap.** Time blindness means the grid is fiction by 10:30. And here's the killer: for an all-or-nothing brain, *a slipped schedule is worse than no schedule* — "the plan is ruined" → abandon the whole day → shame → eventually abandon the app. We would be manufacturing the exact failure mode the user already has with every planner they've ever quit.

**VERDICT: the day-grid dies.**

**Fix:**
- **Plan only the NEXT session, always.** The morning asks two things max: "what matters today?" (one default, pre-selected) and "how many sessions feel realistic — 2, 3, 4?" (a *count*, not clock times). That's the whole plan. The pill offers the next session after each break — a rolling plan that *cannot slip* because it never claimed times.
- The ritual rides ON the chaos, not against it: screen 1 includes "close yesterday's noise?" (the sweep we already built — Prepare stage). Clear the tabs, then one question.
- **Day 1 must work with zero structure** — no goals, no yesterday, no history: "What's the one thing today?" and that's the entire ritual. The 4-screen version only exists once there's something to mirror.

---

## ROUND 2 — 50/10 pomodoro is neurotypical cosplay

**Design as it stands:** 50-min sessions, 10-min breaks, ritual card interrupts at 0:00 to celebrate.

**ADHD reality:** work is bimodal. Either it takes 20 minutes of agony to start and then — if the gods smile — becomes 3 hours of hyperfocus, or it fizzles at minute 15. A timer that **interrupts hyperfocus at minute 50 to show a celebration carousel** is the app serving its own mechanics. Hyperfocus is the single most valuable resource this brain has; the app's job is to PROTECT it, not meter it.

**VERDICT: fixed-length sessions die. Sessions get a floor, not a ceiling.**

**Fix:**
- A session is a **commitment to a minimum** (25 min default — small enough to start), not a box.
- **Flow protection:** at the planned end, if the screen is scoring on-task, the pill says nothing. It rolls into count-up ("1:34 past — still going") and waits for a natural break (app switch, idle, relevance drop) to cash in the celebration. Interruption only ever happens to OFF-task time.
- "+25 more" remains for the fizzle case; ending at minute 25 is a *kept commitment*, never "only 25 minutes."
- The 2pm offer shrinks (25 → even 10): **starting is the only hard part; shrink the ask until starting is possible.**

---

## ROUND 3 — the drift ruler punishes the wrong thing

**Design as it stands:** uniform escalation (90s grace → tint → nudge → soft-close) for any off-task content.

**ADHD reality:** there are TWO drifts and they need opposite responses:
1. **Indulging** (YouTube, X, the feed) — hedonic, knows it's off-task. The fence works. Escalate.
2. **Stuck** (avoidance-scrolling docs, rapid tab thrash, frozen on a blank form) — this is *dysregulation*, usually triggered by one scary subtask (the email they're dreading). **Escalating friction at minute 5 of an anxiety spiral kicks a person who is already down.** They don't need a fence; they need a hand.

**VERDICT: one ruler dies; two responses live.**

**Fix:**
- The coach already distinguishes stuck-vs-drift in the charter — make enforcement honor it. Hedonic drift → the ruler (tint → nudge → soft-close). Stuck patterns (thrash, freeze, avoidance loops) → **rescue, never punishment**: "The cover letter's the scary part? Tiny step: open the doc, type your name. 10 minutes, that's all."
- The smallest-next-physical-action is the rescue's whole vocabulary. Not "get back to work" — "open the file."

---

## ROUND 4 — the reckoning is a shame engine with a report-card UI

**Design as it stands:** evening email to partner/parent rating the day; streaks; "commitment kept ✗" on bad days.

**ADHD reality:** this user has been graded their whole life. A bad day reported to someone they love is not accountability — it's the Sunday-night dread that makes them **delete the app to escape its opinion of them.** Broken streaks are the #1 documented quit-moment in habit apps; for RSD (rejection-sensitive) brains it's worse.

**VERDICT: judgment dies; the mirror and repair live.**

**Fix:**
- The record states, it never grades: "2 sessions, 1h40m, most of the afternoon went sideways" — no ✗, no red, no "failed."
- **Streaks decay, they don't break.** Bad day = streak pauses; two good days restore it. There is no cliff to fall off, so there's nothing to flee.
- **After a bad day the app gets SOFTER, not stricter.** Next morning: "Yesterday was a wash. Happens. One 25 on anything and today's already better." Repair beats judgment — this is also exactly what a good parent says, which IS the parent-teen product.
- The partner email's tone is a designed artifact with the same bench discipline as coach speech (wrong-tone = wrong-speak).

## ROUND 5 — the app itself is a fidget toy

**Design as it stands:** rituals, celebration carousels, chips, a Today page, an evening review, tank numbers on the pill.

**ADHD reality:**
- Every screen we add is itself a dopamine surface to fiddle with. Checking the app is a distraction *we* manufactured. The win condition is the user NOT looking at us.
- "tank −4m · earning nothing" is **on-surface math** (explicitly banned by Ameer's design rules) and rules-lawyer bait. Price lists ("one legal extension per day") teach gaming the system, and ADHD users game it, then resent themselves AND the app.
- Every fork costs spoons. By 2pm even two chips is one chip too many.

**VERDICT: numbers, price-lists, and optional screens die.**

**Fix:**
- Pill shows **states, not math**: `focusing / break / free / unplanned / locked`. The tank number appears only when it's the binding constraint (last 10 min).
- Fewer named rules; coach judgment inside hard caps. No legalese for the user to lawyer.
- **Every prompt has exactly one pre-selected default; Enter/click accepts.** The entire day must be traversable by lazily hitting the same big button. Browsing the app is never required — Today/review exist for the moments they're *wanted*.
- Evening review lives on the **phone** (they're not at the Mac at 9pm; they're in bed with the phone — we know because that's why bedtime lock exists).

---

## ROUND 6 — hyperfocus on the wrong thing, and re-declaring isn't failure

**ADHD classic the design ignored:** 3 hours deep in a side quest (refactoring something irrelevant) scores "focused" because it's code in an editor. The declared focus was job apps.

**Fix:** the coach names it without breaking flow — at the natural pause: "Solid 2h — though it went to the refactor, not job apps. Switch, or make the refactor today's focus?" **Re-declaring is one tap and explicitly not a failure** — the system bends to the day instead of grading the day against the morning's guess. (Same mechanism powers the 1:30 re-plan: mornings lie; correction is cheap.)

---

## ROUND 7 — attack what survived

- *"The morning two-question ritual will get skipped too."* Yes — and that's priced in: skip = unplanned state = the ladder. The ritual is 15 seconds WITH a sweep button precisely so skipping it is the harder habit, not the easier one. Holds.
- *"Auto-start at the ritual is the app deciding."* The block was user-approved; auto-start after a visible 3:00 countdown with Edit present is the agreed default, and starting is the thing they can't do alone. Holds — agency lives at the dial and the plan, not at every 9am.
- *"Flow protection could protect a 6-hour doom-grind that LOOKS like work."* Relevance score + the side-quest naming (Round 6) covers it; coach speaks at the natural pause. Holds.
- *"Repair-over-judgment could excuse everything; teeth vanish."* No: the ladder and the locks are unchanged for hedonic drift (Standard/Strict dial). Softness is only in the *language* and the *next-morning ask*. Enforcement enforces; the coach just stops moralizing. Holds.
- *"Phone evening review = building iOS surface we don't have time for."* v1 = one push notification with the day's one-liner. The full surface waits. Holds.

Nothing else lands. Converged.

## WHAT THE CONVERGED DAY ACTUALLY IS

1. **Wake, phone:** one glance — yesterday + the week, no buttons, no grades.
2. **First sustained Mac use:** *"Close yesterday's noise?"* → sweep → ONE question: "What matters today?" (default pre-selected from week-pace; `🤷` → coach proposes; day-1 = just this question) + "how many sessions feel realistic?" (count). Done in 15–40 seconds.
3. **Rolling sessions:** pill proposes the next session, auto-starts after 3:00. **Floor of 25, no ceiling** — flow is never interrupted; off-task time is the only thing that ever gets friction.
4. **Two drifts, two answers:** indulging → tint → nudge → soft-close → overlay (dial-bound). Stuck → rescue with the smallest physical next action.
5. **Breaks are sanctioned** and bounded by state, not price lists.
6. **Walk away / lid close:** books close cleanly, partial credit, warm re-entry.
7. **Done is declared.** Session count met → "you're free" — the app says STOP, then disappears for the evening.
8. **The mirror, not the report card:** states facts, streaks decay rather than break, bad day → softer morning. Partner sees the same mirror.
9. **The app minimizes its own screen time.** One pre-selected default everywhere; the whole day traversable with one lazy button; no surface that begs to be checked.

**North star that fell out of the loop: the product is not "blocking" or "coaching" — it is a prosthetic for STARTING, a guard for FLOW, and a witness that never shames. Block what indulges, hand what's stuck, protect what's working, forgive what failed, and get out of the way.**
